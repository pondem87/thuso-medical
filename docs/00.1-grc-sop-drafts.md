## Clinician Code of Conduct 
1. Patient Safety First: Provide general insights only; never issue definitive diagnoses or
treatment plans.
2. Respectful Discourse: Maintain professionalism, avoid disparaging colleagues.
3. Evidence-Driven Contributions: Base commentary on accepted clinical guidelines where applicable.
4. Confidentiality: Never introduce identifiable patient information.
5. Transparency: Declare uncertainties and acknowledge differential diagnoses.
6. Boundaries: Do not solicit patients or give private consultations through the platform.
7. Accountability: Accept moderator decisions, participate in audits when requested.

## Moderator Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
1. Intake & Screening
- Review clinician contributions daily.
- Flag unsafe, speculative, or inappropriate commentary.
- Verify discussions do not cross into telemedicine.
2. Synthesis of Client-Facing Summaries
- Summaries must be neutral, concise, and safety-focused.
- Do not present internal disagreements; instead convey structured uncertainty.
- Include red-flag warnings and recommended next steps.
3. Safety & Escalation Protocol
- Immediately escalate any mention of emergencies.
- Temporarily freeze case discussion if risk is identified.
- Notify governance board for repeated clinician violations.
4. Privacy Protection
- Ensure all summaries exclude any identifiable data.
- Reject clinician submissions containing sensitive identifiers.
5. Quality Assurance
- Apply standard templates for summaries.
- Conduct weekly audit reviews.
- Track editing frequency to monitor clinician quality. 
6. Governance & Reporting
- Maintain audit logs.
- Document all escalations.
- Report monthly to clinical governance board.
